FILM

Welcome to my love for film.



Rule #1 in my house: always smile and pose whenever mommy takes her phone out!

The habit of capturing every interesting detail on film echoes my mother’s reminder that each moment is lived only once. The significance of photographs and home videos unfolds as time exerts its influence, our joy hanging like an echo in the years to come. I inherited my mother’s love for filmmaking, and started to pursue it in earnest in 6th grade through vlogs, then it became the love to capture the stories and lives of others.



As I move on, Rule #1 stays etched in my mind—


a commitment to not just document my own life


but also preserve others’ smiles and stories.

Rename

“Rename” follows the story of Zhaodi Xu, an Asian girl whose name carries the weight of a preference for male heirs as it is considered to be “more valuable.” She struggles with the impact of her name and embarks on a journey of self-discovery with the goal of reclaiming a name that truly belongs to her before entering college. This film aims to shed light on the dearth of stories addressing the complex experiences connected to patriarchal names in East Asia and the significant effects they have on several people.

山城少年The Youth of Chongqing

Febraury/2021

Most prominent short film editing work as a hired editor for fan associations, which received 50k views on mainstream Chinese media platforms such as Weibo and Bilibili. This short film "山城 少年 (The Youth of Chongqing)" blends Zhixin Zhu, an Idol trainee's, journey through his hometown, Chongqing, with a vivid depiction of the beauty alluring daily life Chongqingers (people who live in Chongqing) experience of their inhabitants within this mountain city.

Love

March/2023

Promotional film as the main curator for the February 2023 online exhibit, “The Power of Women.” The sound decision of the film to use the Chinese song, “Blank Space” by Tanya Chua, seamlessly enhances the exhibit’s central themes. Through lyrics echoing, “我想你是爱我 的(I think you love me),” it underscores the irony of love that comes with a price and the emotional struggles stemming from Southeast Asian families' conceptions of love, as well as their culture of assigning patriarchal names to their female offsprings that express desire for sons. Conversely, the lyrics “你不再需要给我个答案(You no longer need to give me an answer)” emphasizes our exhibit’s main message of “be yourself before all else,” to encourage more women to embrace their authenticity and to not be trapped in socital constraints.
